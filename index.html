01 — The frame

A partnership-model firm scales consultant AI adoption on a methodology, not on a tool choice.

“Five hundred opinionated advisors each ran a candidate-research workflow that worked before any AI capability existed. Adding tools without a shared context architecture produces uneven adoption, quality drift, and a craft that weakens in the middle.”

The methodology work is sorting the craft into the three layers where work actually gets done. About 60% of what a consultant does on a senior search is database and workflow. Candidate data, schedule coordination, document production. About 30% is rule-based logic. Shortlist gates, reference-check protocols, conflict checks. Around 10% is a genuine AI problem. Judgment on candidate fit, synthesis of conflicting assessment signals, narrative framing of a finalist recommendation.

Sorting those three, before any new tool is rolled out to the 500, is the engagement.
